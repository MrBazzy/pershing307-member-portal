HISTORY-ENHANCEMENTS-001 — History editor, timeline content, and management navigation

Please update the History module with the following changes.

====================================================
1. RICH TEXT EDITING FOR HISTORY CONTENT
====================================================

Current issue:
The History text appears to be plain text only.

Required behavior:
Site Administrator and PM Super Administrator must be able to format History text.

Add a rich text editor or safe formatting toolbar supporting:

- Bold
- Italic
- Underline
- Headings / larger text
- Smaller text
- Font selection if feasible
- Paragraphs
- Bullet lists
- Numbered lists
- Links if safe
- Clear formatting

Security requirements:
- Sanitize stored HTML.
- Do not allow scripts.
- Do not allow unsafe embedded content.
- Prevent XSS.
- Render formatted content safely in the public History view.

Acceptance criteria:
An administrator can make text bold, italic, underlined, larger/smaller, and structure the History page nicely.

====================================================
2. TIMELINE ENTRY CONTENT
====================================================

Current timeline fields:
- Year
- Title
- Description

Please keep this structure, but make it easy to copy/paste formatted historical content into each entry.

Requirements:
- Year field remains separate.
- Title field remains separate.
- Description field supports rich text or at least multi-line formatted text.
- Description must support line breaks and paragraphs.
- Timeline entries should display cleanly in the public History view.

Suggested timeline entries:

1959
Title: Foundation in Fontainebleau
Description:
The Lodge was originally founded by members of the NATO military personnel stationed at NATO Headquarters Fontainebleau, Seine-et-Marne, France.

It was consecrated on 3 October 1959 under the designation "General John J. Pershing Lodge No. 62" under the jurisdiction of the GLNF.

1967
Title: NATO Relocation and Sister Lodge in Maastricht
Description:
When France left NATO's integrated military command structure in 1967, NATO Headquarters Allied Forces Central Europe (AFCENT) relocated to Brunssum, The Netherlands.

A sister Lodge was consecrated on 30 September 1967 in Maastricht under the designation "General John J. Pershing Lodge No. 62 Holland".

1974
Title: Independence of the Dutch Lodge
Description:
On 1 March 1974, the Dutch Lodge became independent of its sister Lodge.

It remained true to its origins and became known as "General John J. Pershing Lodge No. 166 Holland". Only the numerical designation changed from 62 to 166.

2009
Title: Search for a New Jurisdiction
Description:
General John J. Pershing Lodge No. 166 was part of the Continental District of the GLNF until 2009.

Due to loss of international regularity, the members elected to seek membership of the Grootoosten der Nederlanden, the Grand East of the Netherlands.

2012
Title: New Warrant and Consecration as Lodge No. 307
Description:
The Lodge returned its GLNF warrant in 2012.

After three years of negotiation with the Grand East of the Netherlands, the Lodge was consecrated on 3 November 2012 and received a new warrant under the jurisdiction of the GENL.

It became known as "General John J. Pershing Lodge No. 307".

Present
Title: A Unique Multinational Lodge
Description:
General John J. Pershing Lodge No. 307 continues to preserve its unique multinational and military traditions.

The Lodge works the Nova Scotia Ritual by kind permission of the Grand Lodge of Nova Scotia and remains the only Lodge in Europe known to do so.

====================================================
3. MOVE HISTORY EDITING TO MANAGEMENT SECTION
====================================================

Current issue:
History editing is not clearly separated from the public History view.

Required behavior:
There must be two separate areas:

A. Public / member-facing navigation:
- History

This is the normal read-only History view.
It remains visible to all allowed users.

B. Management section:
- History Management

This appears only inside the Management square/block for:
- Site Administrator
- PM Super Administrator

Administrators should be able to:
- Open History Management
- Edit History content
- Edit Timeline entries
- Save changes
- Then open the normal History page to immediately see the result

Requirements:
- Do not expose edit controls in the public History view.
- Normal users must never see edit buttons.
- Keep History view and History Management clearly separated.
- Add audit logging for:
  - History content updated
  - Timeline entry created
  - Timeline entry updated
  - Timeline entry deleted/reordered

Acceptance criteria:
- History remains visible as a normal read-only page.
- History Management appears inside the Management section only.
- Site Administrator and PM Super Administrator can edit content.
- Normal members and visitors cannot edit content.
