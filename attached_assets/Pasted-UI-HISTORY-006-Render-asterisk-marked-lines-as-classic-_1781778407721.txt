UI-HISTORY-006 — Render asterisk-marked lines as classic chapter headings

I have updated the Our History text so that every chapter heading starts with an asterisk "*".

Example source text:

* Origins in NATO Europe (1959)

Required rendering behavior on the public Our History page:

1. Detect headings
- Any line or paragraph starting with "*" must be treated as a chapter heading.

2. Remove asterisk
- Do not display the "*" in the public view.

3. Extract date/year
- Extract the year or date range between brackets.
- Examples:
  - (1959)
  - (2009–2012)
  - (1959 – Present)

4. Move date to the front
Display the extracted date/year before the heading title.

Example:

[1959] Origins in NATO Europe

5. Date badge styling
The extracted year/date should appear:
- in a small square or badge
- Masonic Gold border
- warm ivory background
- gold or midnight-blue text
- visually aligned with the heading

6. Heading styling
The heading title should:
- start with a capital letter
- use an elegant classic book-style heading font
- use Midnight Blue
- not be underlined
- not look like a hyperlink
- feel like a chapter heading in a classic historical book

7. Remove brackets from title
The date/year must not remain at the end of the heading.

Example source:
* Origins in NATO Europe (1959)

Rendered:
[1959] Origins in NATO Europe

8. Divider before each heading
Before each detected chapter heading, show the elegant divider.

Update the divider:
- thin horizontal line
- three small centered Masonic Gold diamonds instead of one
- good vertical spacing
- restrained and classic, not flashy

9. Hero watermark
Use the uploaded Square & Compasses + G symbol as the hero watermark.
- Very low opacity: 3–5%
- Centered behind the hero title
- Must not distract from the text

10. Preserve
Do not change:
- stored text content
- database schema
- routes
- permissions
- History Management editor
- Timeline page
- Historical Documents page

Goal:
Administrators can mark chapter headings by starting a line with "*", and the public Our History page automatically renders those lines as elegant classic-book chapter headings.
